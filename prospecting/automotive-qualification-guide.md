# Automotive Qualification Guide

This guide explains how to evaluate whether a founder‑led automotive service business in Greater Manchester is a good fit for SignalScan.  It draws on typical characteristics of independent garages, MOT centres and vehicle specialists.

## Ideal Client Traits

1. **Size and Structure** – The business has between 2 and 30 staff, is owner‑managed and does not have an in‑house cybersecurity team.
2. **Service Value** – Each customer interaction represents meaningful revenue (e.g. MOT, major repair or specialist service), making conversion improvements valuable.
3. **Customer Acquisition** – The business relies on inbound enquiries (phone calls, form submissions, quote requests) rather than purely walk‑in traffic.
4. **Digital Maturity** – The website and digital journey exist but are outdated, unclear or underperforming.  There may be no dedicated marketing or IT support.
5. **Reviews and Reputation** – There are recent public reviews on Google or other platforms, indicating active customers.  Reviews may highlight strengths or reveal recurring concerns.
6. **Trust Gaps** – The site may lack clear trust signals, privacy notices or secure forms.  Owners may not be aware of basic cyber hygiene.
7. **Decision Agility** – The owner is identifiable and can authorise improvements quickly.  There is a willingness to invest modestly in trust and customer experience.

## Red Flags

1. **Large Corporate Chain** – Multi‑location or corporate franchises often have centralised marketing and security.  They are less likely to value an external trust scan.
2. **No Web Presence** – Businesses without a website or online booking journey will not benefit from a surface‑based assessment.
3. **Regulatory Focus** – If the prospect requests formal penetration testing or compliance certification, clarify that SignalScan does not provide this.
4. **Price Sensitivity** – If typical service value is low (e.g. <£50) the commercial impact of improvements may not justify the investment.

## Conversation Starters

Use the insights from your research to craft personalised messages.  For example:

> “I noticed your online booking form requires multiple pages on mobile.  Our 48‑hour assessment can reveal quick wins to improve conversions and trust.”

> “Recent reviews praise your honesty but your website doesn’t feature testimonials.  We can help identify similar opportunities.”

Position the service as a supportive, non‑intrusive way to understand what prospects, competitors and opportunists see and to prioritise improvements.
