# Testimonial & Case Study Process

Client testimonials and case studies provide social proof but must be obtained ethically and with clear consent.

## 1. Eligibility

- Only clients who have completed a full SignalScan are eligible to provide a testimonial.
- The founding offer includes permission to request (not require) a testimonial.
- Positive testimonials are not mandatory; honest feedback is encouraged.

## 2. Timing

- Wait until after the client has reviewed the report and implemented at least some recommendations.
- Follow up within two weeks to request feedback and a testimonial.

## 3. Requesting a Testimonial

1. Thank the client for their trust and ask whether they would be willing to share a brief testimonial about their experience.
2. Provide a set of guiding questions, such as:
   - What problem were you hoping to solve?
   - How did SignalScan help you understand your digital presence?
   - What changes have you made based on the report?
3. Make it clear they can decline or edit any quoted material.

## 4. Using Testimonials

- Obtain written permission to publish the testimonial on your website or marketing materials.  Specify where and how it will be used.
- Do not fabricate or exaggerate statements.  Use the client’s own words.
- If the client prefers anonymity, use only their role and industry (e.g. “Owner, Independent MOT Centre in Greater Manchester”).

## 5. Case Studies

1. For a deeper case study, ask the client if they would be comfortable sharing more detail about their improvements and outcomes.
2. Draft the case study and share it with the client for approval before publication.
3. Exclude sensitive details and focus on the process and qualitative improvements (e.g. “increased enquiries after fixing trust gaps”).

## 6. Withdrawal and Updates

- The client may withdraw their testimonial or case study at any time.  Honour such requests promptly.
- Update published materials to reflect any changes.

Following this process builds trust and ensures testimonials are authentic, respectful and legally compliant.
