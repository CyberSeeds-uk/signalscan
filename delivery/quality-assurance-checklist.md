# Quality Assurance Checklist

Before delivering a SignalScan report, use this checklist to ensure accuracy, clarity and adherence to ethical boundaries.

## Content and Structure

- [ ] Report includes all required sections (cover, confidentiality, scope, executive judgement, method, surface map, findings, priority matrix, actions, limitations, etc.).
- [ ] Findings are grouped by SIGNAL stage with unique IDs.
- [ ] Each finding includes observation, interpretation and recommendation, and clearly differentiates between them.
- [ ] Evidence is cited with timestamps and source URLs.  Sensitive evidence is marked appropriately.
- [ ] No unsupported claims (e.g. promises of revenue, rankings or compliance).
- [ ] Pricing and guarantee statements are accurate.

## Language and Tone

- [ ] Language is clear, professional and free from jargon.
- [ ] Tone is empowering and trauma‑aware; no shame or blame.
- [ ] Recommendations are actionable without implying intrusion or exploitation.
- [ ] Ethical and legal boundaries are restated where necessary.

## Formatting

- [ ] Headings, tables and lists do not break awkwardly across pages (check print preview).
- [ ] No broken links or missing images.
- [ ] Confidentiality marking appears on the cover and footer.
- [ ] Page numbers or footers are consistent if included.

## Consistency

- [ ] Terminology (e.g. “SignalScan”, “Three‑Signal Preview”) is used consistently.
- [ ] Dates and times are accurate and use the correct format (e.g. 9 June 2026).
- [ ] Client and competitor names are spelled correctly and are fictionalised if necessary.

## Data Protection

- [ ] Personal data is not included unless strictly necessary and, if present, is redacted or anonymised.
- [ ] Evidence does not contain sensitive personal information or exploit details.
- [ ] Retention and deletion instructions are included.

Completing this checklist helps maintain high standards and protects both the client and Jxnesyy Signal Lab.
