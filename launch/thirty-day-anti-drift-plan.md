# Thirty‑Day Anti‑Drift Plan

Launching a new service is exciting, but it’s easy to lose momentum after the initial push.  This 30‑day plan ensures you continue to iterate, market and deliver SignalScan without drifting away from your goals.

## Week 1

- Execute the seven‑day revenue sprint and onboard the first clients.
- Gather structured feedback from early previews and reports.
- Refine the report template and tooling based on real‑world use.

## Week 2

- Analyse the performance of outreach channels (email, LinkedIn, Instagram, phone).  Double down on what works.
- Publish a case study or testimonial (with permission) from the first client.
- Reach out to local business networks and trade groups for partnership opportunities.
- Begin development of SignalWatch service outline and pricing for recurring monitoring.

## Week 3

- Schedule retests or follow‑ups with early clients to measure improvements and upsell SignalRepair where appropriate.
- Update the website with any new content (e.g. Q&A from real clients, resources).
- Review internal processes, data handling and evidence storage.  Conduct a mini audit against the quality and retention policies.
- Expand prospecting beyond Greater Manchester if local uptake is limited, focusing on similar founder‑led automotive businesses.

## Week 4

- Evaluate financial performance: number of clients, revenue, cost per acquisition.
- Revisit pricing and offer structure; adjust if necessary based on feedback and perceived value.
- Plan a small webinar or workshop on digital trust for small garages, offering value and gently promoting SignalScan.
- Set goals for the next 90 days and identify any skills, resources or partnerships needed to achieve them.

By following this anti‑drift plan you will maintain momentum, learn from early engagements and build a sustainable pipeline of clients.
