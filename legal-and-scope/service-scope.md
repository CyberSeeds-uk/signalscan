# Service Scope – SignalScan

This document defines what is included and excluded in a SignalScan engagement.  It should be shared with the client during scope confirmation to set expectations and ensure mutual understanding.

## Included Activities

- **Public Surface Mapping** – Identifying the primary domain, confirmed related domains and subdomains, indexed pages, public business profiles, social accounts and public contact routes.
- **Passive Technical Observation** – Reviewing TLS availability, certificate details, HTTP response headers, redirect chains, public DNS records (A, AAAA, MX, TXT, NS), and the presence of SPF, DKIM (when a selector is provided) and DMARC records.
- **Privacy and Policy Observation** – Checking for privacy notices, cookie banners, consent dialogs and other publicly visible compliance indicators.
- **Customer Journey Review** – Walking through the mobile customer journey without submitting personal data or triggering business workflows.  Identifying friction, dead ends and unclear calls to action.
- **Narrative Assessment** – Analysing recent public reviews, testimonials, stated qualifications and trust claims.
- **Competitor Comparison** – Comparing three client‑approved competitor sites on surface clarity, trust indicators and customer experience.
- **Priority Matrix and Recommendations** – Providing a prioritised list of findings and suggested actions, with clear distinction between observed facts and subjective interpretation.

## Excluded Activities

- **Penetration Testing** – No exploitation of vulnerabilities, brute‑forcing, port scanning, directory enumeration or intrusive vulnerability scanning.
- **Access to Private Systems** – We do not access client or third‑party systems beyond what is publicly available.
- **Social Engineering** – No attempts to deceive staff or customers in order to obtain information.
- **Compliance Certification** – SignalScan does not provide regulatory compliance attestation or legal advice.
- **Guarantees** – We do not guarantee revenue, rankings, leads or absolute security.  We report on what we observe and interpret; outcomes depend on many factors outside our control.

## Client Responsibilities

- Provide accurate and complete information on domains, subdomains and public profiles to be assessed.
- Obtain any necessary permissions from stakeholders to permit our passive review.
- Refrain from making changes to the public digital surface during the 48‑hour assessment window unless necessary for security or operations.
- Review and act upon the recommendations according to your own judgement and capabilities, or engage SignalRepair for assistance.

This scope forms part of the engagement terms and should be acknowledged before work begins.
