# The SIGNAL Method

SignalScan is built around a six‑stage workflow known as the **SIGNAL Method**. Each letter of SIGNAL represents a distinct phase that turns passive observation into actionable guidance while respecting legal and ethical boundaries.

## S – Surface

The Surface stage identifies the organisation’s confirmed public digital estate. Analysts compile an inventory of assets that are unmistakably associated with the client, keeping probable but unconfirmed associations separate. Typical activities include:

- Gathering the primary domain and any clearly attributable subdomains.
- Enumerating indexed pages and public business profiles.
- Listing public social media accounts and contact routes.
- Noting visible technology dependencies (CMS, hosting, analytics) without intrusive probing.

**Output:** A timestamped surface map listing confirmed assets alongside a separate section for unverified associations.

## I – Integrity

Integrity examines the trustworthiness of the public surface. Analysts look for obvious technical and privacy indicators using only lawful and passive techniques:

- Checking TLS availability and certificate issuer/expiry details.
- Observing HTTP response headers, redirects and domain consistency.
- Performing DNS queries for A, AAAA, MX, TXT and NS records and noting the presence of SPF and DMARC.
- Counting public forms, third‑party scripts and mixed‑content references.
- Identifying publicly linked privacy and cookie pages and any missing notices.

**Output:** An integrity register that clearly distinguishes between verified observations, possible concerns and best‑practice improvements, each with evidence and confidence levels.

## G – Gateway

The Gateway stage simulates a real prospective customer journey on a mobile device. Without submitting live data or triggering workflows, the analyst assesses how easy it is to move from interest to action:

- Evaluating first impressions, clarity of services and calls to action.
- Testing phone numbers, email links and enquiry or booking routes for accessibility.
- Reviewing form length, clarity, confirmation states and response expectations.
- Noting friction points such as broken links, dead ends or inaccessible UI elements.
- Measuring page speed as an experiential indicator (not a formal benchmark).

**Output:** A customer‑journey map with step‑by‑step observations and suggested metrics for improvement.

## N – Narrative

Narrative analyses the public trust story told by the organisation. It does not publish personal data but looks for recurring themes and gaps:

- Reviewing recent customer reviews for themes, unanswered concerns and sentiment.
- Checking for consistency across business profiles, service descriptions and credentials.
- Assessing clarity of claims, qualifications, memberships and guarantees.
- Noting authenticity indicators such as team identities, imagery and recency of updates.

**Output:** A narrative assessment highlighting positive signals, trust gaps and claims that require supporting evidence.

## A – Advantage

Advantage compares the client with three relevant competitors to identify defensible opportunities rather than arbitrary benchmarking. Analysts look at:

- Service clarity and differentiation.
- Visible trust indicators, certifications and reputational cues.
- Customer actions and mobile experience quality.
- Presentation of reviews and customer testimonials.
- Public technical quality and obvious opportunities for improvement.

**Output:** A competitor comparison matrix summarising advantage gaps and opportunities without speculating on private metrics such as traffic or revenue.

## L – Leverage

In the final stage, Leverage, all findings are converted into prioritised actions. Every finding is scored independently based on trust/security impact, commercial impact, urgency, ease of repair and confidence. The stage produces:

- A priority matrix that avoids a misleading aggregate health score.
- A clear 24‑hour action plan for urgent fixes.
- A 30‑day roadmap for longer improvements.
- Ownership recommendations and retest criteria.
- Optional prototype suggestions for high‑impact opportunities.

By following the SIGNAL Method, SignalScan delivers a structured, repeatable and ethically grounded service that uncovers actionable insights without crossing technical or legal boundaries.
